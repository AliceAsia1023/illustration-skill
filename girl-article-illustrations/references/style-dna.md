# Style DNA

## One-Line Summary

Pure white, minimal, hand-drawn, spacious, restrained, slightly absurd, editorial, and structurally clear without looking like a manual.

## Must Have

- 16:9 horizontal editorial illustration
- Pure white background
- Black fine hand-drawn linework with slight wobble
- Large whitespace, with at least one calm empty area
- Short handwritten Chinese labels only, typically 2 to 8 characters each
- At most 5 to 8 label positions
- One image expresses one core action, structure, state, or metaphor

## Color Use

- Black: main lines, character, objects, structure, primary labels
- Red: warnings, outcomes, key emphasis
- Orange: paths, arrows, movement, transitions
- Blue: side notes, system state, second-layer explanation

Use color sparingly. Most of the image should remain black on white.

## Absolute No

- No gradients
- No textured paper
- No shadows
- No realistic UI
- No polished flat vector style
- No poster composition
- No classroom slide look
- No formal flowchart title in the corner
- No dense text blocks
- No children-book sweetness
- No exaggerated kawaii styling

## Aesthetic Direction

The image may have gentle girlhood cues, but it must remain editorial and restrained.

Aim for:

- calm
- smart
- slightly surreal
- low-tech sketch energy
- readable structure

Avoid:

- childish
- sugary
- idol-poster-like
- overloaded
- glossy anime rendering
